Hyppopotamus

  Hippos and whales shared a common semiaquatic ancestor that branched off from other artiodactyls around 60 million years ago

  • Binomial Name: Hippopotamus amphibius
  • Average Weight: 3,310 lb (Adults)
  • Average Lifespan: 40 to 50 years
  • Habitat: Rivers, lakes, mangrove swamps

  The Hippopotamus, also called the hippo, common hippopotamus or river hippopotamus, is a large, mostly herbivorous, and semiaquatic mammal. Hippos inhabit rivers, lakes, and mangrove swamps
